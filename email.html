What is Email?

Email, or electronic mail, is a fast and easy way to send messages, files, and documents over the internet. It's widely used for personal and professional communication.

How Does Email Work?

Emails are sent from your account to someone else's using the internet. Once sent, the message is stored in the recipient's inbox, ready for them to read and reply.

Main Parts of an Email

  • Sender: The person writing and sending the email.
  • Recipient: The person receiving the email.
  • Subject: A short title summarizing the email's purpose.
  • Body: The main message or content of the email.
  • Attachments: Extra files, like documents or pictures, sent with the email.

Why Use Email?

  • Fast Communication: Messages are delivered almost instantly.
  • Free to Use: Send unlimited messages without any postage costs.
  • Share Files Easily: Attach and share important documents or images.
  • Keeps Records: Emails automatically save your conversations for future reference.

Stay Safe While Using Email

  • Create Strong Passwords: Protect your email account with secure passwords.
  • Avoid Scams: Do not click on unknown links or open suspicious attachments.
  • Use Two-Factor Authentication: Add extra security by requiring a code along with your password.
