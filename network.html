Networks

Networks connect devices like computers, phones, or printers to share data and resources. They make it easier for us to work together, communicate, and manage information efficiently.

Types of Networks

  • LAN: Covers a small area, like your home, school, or office. For example, your Wi-Fi at home is part of a LAN.
  • WAN: Covers a much larger area, like cities or countries. The internet itself is the biggest WAN!
  • MAN: Covers areas like a city or university campus, linking multiple LANs together.

Important Network Devices

  • Router: Like a traffic cop, it directs data between networks and connects you to the internet.
  • Switch: Manages data sharing between devices in a network, like your computer and printer.
  • Access Point: Lets your phone or laptop connect wirelessly to the network.
  • Server: A computer that stores files, applications, or services for others to use.

Why Networks Are Useful

  • Sharing Resources: Share printers, files, and even your internet connection with others.
  • Faster Communication: Send emails, messages, or files instantly between devices.
  • Saves Money: Share one printer or storage device instead of buying separate ones for each user.
  • Easy Expansion: Add more devices to the network as your needs grow.
